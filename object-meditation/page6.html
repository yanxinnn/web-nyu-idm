I had a lot of thoughts of quitting, starting from the very first practice.

During the runs, I told myself that this one would be my last. That I'd tell the coaches that I quit.

But for some reason, I never did.

Maybe it was because I loved the soreness and tiredness I felt after a run,

the amount of ice cream I could eat without gaining weight

the celebratory team sweets buffet after a race

the confidence that I gained from being in shape.

I started to run not to just survive the run, but to improve myself.

It was so satisfying to see the fruits of my efforts and improving my personal best race times. Before I knew it, my XC seasons were over.
