I had a lot of thoughts

of quiting, starting from

the very first practice.

During the runs, I told myself

that this one would be my last.

That I'd tell the coaches that I quit.

But for some reason,

I never did.

Maybe it was because I loved

the soreness and tiredness

I felt after a run,

the celebratory team

sweets feasts after a race,

the confidence that I gained.

I started to run not to

just survive the run

but to improve myself.

It was so satisfying to see

the fruits of my efforts

and improvement in my race times.

Before I knew it,

my XC seasons were over.
